# Second Brain Product Principles

## North Star

Second Brain is a governed documentation compiler that produces stage-gated, source-arbitrated, junior-engineer-executable artifact sets with claim-level traceability.

## Core Differentiator

Second Brain should not compete on generic enterprise AI capabilities such as chat, memory, search, app actions, or generic multi-agent automation.

It should specialize in the governance-and-closure band:

- Source authority and domain tagging
- Claim-level conflict resolution
- Vendor truth validation
- Section-level citation discipline
- Inspectable retrieval before generation
- Stage-gated technical-document production
- Junior-engineer-executable closure
- Project artifact lifecycle management

## Non-Negotiables

1. Raw sources remain immutable.
2. Canonical knowledge must have provenance.
3. Transcripts are evidence, not authority.
4. Vendor capability claims must be validated against vendor sources.
5. Generated documentation must be self-sufficient enough for a junior engineer to execute.
6. Retrieval and source selection must be inspectable.
7. Published artifacts must meet closure rules.
8. Agents may assist judgment but must not silently bypass governance.
9. Human review should focus on meaningful product and authority decisions, not low-level cleanup.

## Design Biases

Prefer:

- Explicit source authority over implicit model confidence.
- Claim records over loose summaries.
- Draft ADRs over silent roadmap mutation.
- Experiments over speculative adoption.
- Local Markdown and Git inspectability over opaque state.
- Structured adjudication over generic multi-agent debate.

Avoid:

- Treating transcripts as normal RAG sources for published artifacts.
- Allowing meeting talk to mutate standards.
- Building generic enterprise AI workbench features.
- Optimizing for autonomy at the expense of governance.
- Adding complex infrastructure before the workflow proves valuable.
